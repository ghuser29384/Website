Main page

This is not a travel guide or a live crisis tracker. It is a coarse effective-altruist map of where preventable suffering concentrates and where institutions can move the largest outcomes.

Dominant lens
Why it rises

Scale

Big harms stay big even when they are ordinary.

Recurring disease, chronic pollution, and factory farming can matter more than the dramatic event your attention system prefers.

Leverage

Some regions matter because of institutions, not local burden.

Rich regions often surface governance and policy questions because decisions made there spill outward across the world.

Neglect

The map prefers what is under-seen.

Many of the largest problems are familiar enough to look normal. That is part of why they remain under-addressed.

Grounding

What informed the map

The framing comes from Bentham’s Bulldog and the Effective Altruism Forum. The regional issue choices are illustrative, with broad factual grounding from WHO and Our World in Data rather than a claim to be a live ranked dashboard.
